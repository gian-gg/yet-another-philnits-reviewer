# 2022S_FE_AM_46

Which ACID property guarantees that once a transaction has committed, its
effects will persist even in the event of a system failure?

a) Atomicity
b) Consistency
c) Isolation
d) Durability
?
d) Durability

### Explanation

**Durability** ensures committed changes survive crashes, typically via
write-ahead logging and fsync'd log records. The system can recover by
replaying the log after restart.

### Why the Others Won't Fit

**a) Atomicity**:

- Guarantees a transaction is all-or-nothing, but says nothing about persistence
  after commit.

**b) Consistency**:

- Ensures the database moves from one valid state to another per defined
  constraints.

**c) Isolation**:

- Controls visibility of concurrent transactions, not post-commit persistence.
