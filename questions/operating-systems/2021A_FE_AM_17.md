# 2021A_FE_AM_17

Under round-robin CPU scheduling, what happens to a process that has just
used its full time quantum without finishing?

a) It is terminated immediately.
b) It is moved to the head of the ready queue.
c) It is preempted and moved to the tail of the ready queue.
d) It is promoted to a higher priority class.
?
c) It is preempted and moved to the tail of the ready queue.

### Explanation

Round-robin (RR) scheduling gives each ready process a fixed time quantum.
When the quantum expires, the running process is preempted and re-enqueued
at the **tail** of the ready queue, and the scheduler dispatches the next
process at the head. This yields fair CPU sharing among ready processes.

### Why the Others Won't Fit

**a) Terminated immediately**:

- RR does not terminate processes on quantum expiration; it only preempts.

**b) Head of the ready queue**:

- Placing it at the head would starve other processes and defeat fairness.

**d) Promoted to a higher priority**:

- That's a multilevel feedback policy, not round-robin.
