# 2021A_FE_AM_39

Which cryptographic property ensures that a message has not been altered in
transit?

a) Confidentiality
b) Integrity
c) Availability
d) Non-repudiation
?
b) Integrity

### Explanation

**Integrity** guarantees that a message has not been modified. It is usually
enforced with a **MAC (Message Authentication Code)** or a **digital
signature**, where the receiver recomputes a tag or hash and compares.

### Why the Others Won't Fit

**a) Confidentiality**:

- Hides the contents from unauthorized parties; encryption alone does not
  detect modification.

**c) Availability**:

- Ensures services and data are reachable when needed — unrelated to content
  tampering.

**d) Non-repudiation**:

- Prevents the sender from denying having sent a message; integrity is a
  prerequisite but not the same property.
